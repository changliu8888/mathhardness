Sports agents do not typically create their own high school rankings; instead, they evaluate and rank prospects by heavily relying on established scouting services, evaluating on-field metrics, assessing marketability, and gathering feedback from trusted high school and college coaches.Agents evaluate and prioritize high school athletes using these primary methods:Scouting Services & Consensus Rankings: Agents monitor databases like 247Sports, On3, and ESPN to analyze star ratings and positional rankings, focusing on consensus top-tier recruits who will draw the most lucrative Name, Image, and Likeness (NIL) or professional offers.Physical Metrics & Measurables: Agents and their staff record or request specific biomechanical data, such as height, wingspan, speed (e.g., 40-yard dash for football, vertical leap for basketball), and overall physical development.On-Field Performance & Film: They utilize video coordinators to review game footage, looking for technical proficiency, basketball/football IQ, and consistent production against elite competition.Marketability & "The Brand": Since a large part of an agent's business involves endorsements, they rank athletes based on social media presence, personal charisma, and local or national appeal.Coaching Feedback: Agents maintain deep networks of high school, AAU, and college coaches. 
They rely on these insiders for character evaluations, work ethic, and "coachability".
